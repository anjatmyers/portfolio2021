About Me

Who am I?

I am a Junior Full-stack Software Engineer keeping my skills sharp through freelance website construction. I graduated from DigitalCrafts Intensive Coding Bootcamp March 2021. I am seeking out an entry-level position where I can continue to grow as a developer in a team-based environment, ideally, with mentorship available.

I am located in: Atlanta, GA

Where am I coming from?

I graduated from the University of Virginia in 2017 with a degree in public health. Most of my experience is in the medical field. I've worked as an EMT, medical assistant, and scribe. In 2020, I decided to make a transition from the medical field to software engineering.

Why am I here?

My first insight into the importance of thoughtfully designed technology came when I was working closely with EMR software (electronic medical records). The systems I worked with were nonintuitive, awkward to navigate, and made my job harder in many ways. This inspired me to explore software engineering. While I may no longer work directly with patients, I am still very passionate about providing tools that make people's everyday lives easier.

Why am I a good fit?

Premed coursework and patient exposure prepped me for a different career path but the skills I learned along the way are well suited to software development. Over the years, I have honed many soft skills that stem from working in service of others. These include a sharp attention to detail, strong work ethic, empathetic communication, and the ability to listen to the needs of others and display clear understanding.

What is my motivation?

I'm excited to continue my growth as a developer in hopes that I can make technology more functional and accessible to people that need it the most.
